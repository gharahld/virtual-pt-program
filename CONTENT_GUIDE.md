## üçº Exercise Programs by Age

### Ages 0‚Äì3 Months: Head Control & Early Strength

**Focus**: Building foundational strength and head control

**Exercises**:

1. **Tummy Time Play**
   - Purpose: Neck, shoulder, and core strength
   - Developmental Skills: Head lifting, arm support
   - Duration: 3-5 minutes, gradually increasing
   - Key Safety: Never on soft surfaces, constant supervision

2. **Side-Lying Play**
   - Purpose: Midline orientation and rolling readiness
   - Developmental Skills: Hands to midline, trunk activation
   - Duration: 5 minutes per side
   - Key Safety: Stable support with rolled towel

3. **Supported Sitting with Head Control**
   - Purpose: Neck stability and visual tracking
   - Developmental Skills: Head control, visual-motor integration
   - Duration: Short sessions during alert times
   - Key Safety: Full trunk support required

### Ages 3‚Äì6 Months: Rolling & Sitting Balance

**Focus**: Transitional movements and trunk control

**Exercises**:

1. **Rolling Practice**
   - Purpose: Trunk strength and transitional movements
   - Developmental Skills: Segmental rolling, rotation
   - Duration: Multiple brief attempts
   - Key Safety: Gentle guidance, never force

2. **Tummy Time on Arms**
   - Purpose: Upper body strength for crawling preparation
   - Developmental Skills: Shoulder stability, spinal extension
   - Duration: Increasing time as tolerated
   - Key Safety: Monitor arm positioning

3. **Supported Sitting Play**
   - Purpose: Trunk control and balance
   - Developmental Skills: Postural control, balance reactions
   - Duration: Several minutes with support
   - Key Safety: Hands close for catching

### Ages 6‚Äì12 Months: Mobility, Standing & Transitions

**Focus**: Advanced mobility and walking preparation

**Exercises**:

1. **Sitting to Hands-and-Knees Transitions**
   - Purpose: Crawling position practice
   - Developmental Skills: Weight shifting, transitional movement
   - Duration: Multiple transitions throughout day
   - Key Safety: Clear floor space

2. **Supported Standing at Furniture**
   - Purpose: Leg strength and balance
   - Developmental Skills: Lower extremity weight bearing
   - Duration: Brief standing periods
   - Key Safety: Stable furniture, close supervision

3. **Crawling or Cruising Games**
   - Purpose: Coordination and independence
   - Developmental Skills: Reciprocal movement, dynamic balance
   - Duration: Play-based throughout day
   - Key Safety: Safe play area, remove hazards

## üìö Educational Resources

### Why Home Exercise Programs Matter

**Key Messages**:
- Personalized activity plans created by pediatric PTs
- Infants learn through repetition and play
- Short daily practice is most effective
- Empowers caregivers to participate actively

**Benefits Highlighted**:
1. Promotes healthy motor development
2. Encourages strength through play
3. Supports brain development
4. Improves therapy carryover
5. Provides early intervention support
6. Builds caregiver confidence

### Virtual vs. Traditional Handouts

**Virtual Advantages**:
- Video demonstrations for clarity
- Accessible anytime, anywhere
- Progress tracking tools
- Easy updates as child grows
- Improved caregiver confidence
- Telehealth support integration
- Engaging interactive formats

**When Paper Handouts Work**:
- Limited technology access
- Prefer printed references
- Backup documentation
- No internet required

## üö® When to Contact a Physical Therapist

### Red Flags for Parents
- Consistently dislikes tummy time
- Very stiff or very floppy movement
- Favors one side significantly
- Not meeting major movement milestones
- Difficulty with sitting, crawling, or standing
- Loss of previously acquired skills

### Message to Parents
"Early support makes a big difference! Don't hesitate to reach out to your pediatrician or physical therapist with concerns."

## üí° Tips for Caregivers

### Daily Practice Guidelines
- ‚úì Keep activities playful and short
- ‚úì Follow your baby's lead
- ‚úì Practice during daily routines
- ‚úì Clear the floor for safety
- ‚úì Celebrate every small success
- ‚úì Contact therapist with questions

### Session Structure
- **Duration**: 5-15 minutes total
- **Frequency**: 1-3 times daily
- **Timing**: During alert, happy periods
- **Environment**: Safe, clear floor space
- **Supervision**: Constant adult presence

### Signs to Stop Exercise
- Baby shows distress or crying
- Signs of fatigue or sleepiness
- Immediately after feeding
- Baby resists or pushes away
- Any pain indicators
